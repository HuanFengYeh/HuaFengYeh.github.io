About

Hello! I’m Huanfeng Yeh. My journey into Computer Science began during my time as a research assistant during my undergraduate experience at National Changhua University of Education, where I developed robotic systems for students with autism. This experience showed me how technology could be a bridge, enabling better communication and learning for individuals with diverse needs. As I worked through hardware challenges, I realized that software offered even greater flexibility and scalability to address these challenges on a larger, more impactful scale. This is what led me to shift from Electrical Engineering to Computer Science—software's potential to create adaptable, personalized solutions for a wider range of users.

During my Master’s program at Northeastern University, I first encountered AI accessibility through my research on generative AI chatbots in educational settings. I was fascinated by how AI could assist with personalized learning, but I also saw where these tools often fall short, especially for users with specific accessibility needs. This realization sparked a deep interest in ensuring that AI technologies are designed inclusively, so they can serve all users, not just those who fit typical user profiles. It became clear to me that inclusivity must be at the core of technological innovation, as the impact of AI can only be fully realized when it’s truly for everyone.

In my current role at MAPLE, a nonprofit platform for submitting legislative testimony, I’ve seen firsthand how accessibility can either empower or hinder users. Many individuals—especially those from underrepresented communities—struggle to engage with digital platforms when they aren’t designed with accessibility in mind. This has reinforced my belief that creating inclusive technologies isn’t just a technical challenge; it’s a social responsibility.

As I pursue a PhD, I am eager to continue addressing these challenges, collaborating with researchers to develop AI systems that are not only innovative but also equitable and accessible to all. I'm particularly interested in exploring how AI can enhance accessibility through features like improved alt text generation. The potential for AI to automatically create detailed, context-aware image descriptions excites me, as it could significantly improve the digital experience for users with visual impairments. However, I'm also mindful of the challenges this presents, such as ensuring AI-generated content is free from bias and accurately represents complex or culturally-specific imagery.

I believe that by combining AI's capabilities with human oversight and customization, we can create more inclusive digital environments that cater to diverse user needs. This approach aligns with my goal of developing AI systems that bridge gaps and empower all users, regardless of their abilities or backgrounds."
